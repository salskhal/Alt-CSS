FAQs

URL shortening works by taking a long URL and creating a shorter, condensed version that redirects to the original URL. When a user clicks on the shortened link, they are redirected to the intended destination.
Account requirements vary by service. While some URL shorteners allow anonymous use, creating an account often provides additional features like link management and analytics.
Link permanence depends on the service provider. Some offer permanent links, while others may expire after a certain period or if unused. Premium accounts often ensure permanent links.
Limitations vary by service. Free accounts may have daily or monthly limits, while premium accounts typically offer unlimited URL shortening.
Many services offer custom URL options, allowing you to create branded or memorable links. This feature may be limited to premium accounts.
Most URL shortening services provide basic analytics including click counts. Premium features may include detailed metrics like geographic data and referral sources.
Reputable URL shortening services implement security measures to prevent spam and malicious use. This includes link scanning and suspicious activity monitoring.
A QR code is a two-dimensional barcode that can store data like URLs, text, or contact information. When scanned with a smartphone camera, it quickly provides access to the encoded information.
